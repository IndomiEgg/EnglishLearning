Important Grammar Rules with Examples

1. Subject-Verb Agreement

Rule: Singular subjects use singular verbs; plural subjects use plural verbs.

Example: She runs fast. / They run fast.

2. Articles (a, an, the)

Rule: Use 'a' before consonant sounds, 'an' before vowel sounds, 'the' for specific things.

Example: I saw a cat. / He ate an apple. / She read the book.

3. Modal Verbs

Rule: Use modal verbs to express ability, permission, requests, or possibility.

Example: She can swim. / May I come in?

4. Prepositions

Rule: Prepositions show relationships in time, place, direction, etc.

Example: The book is on the table. / He came from London.

5. Conditional Sentences

Rule: Use conditionals to talk about possible or hypothetical situations.

Example: If it rains, we will stay home.

6. Passive Voice

Rule: Use passive voice to emphasize the action or the object of a sentence.

Example: The cake was eaten by the children.

7. Relative Clauses

Rule: Use relative clauses to add extra information about a noun.

Example: The man who called you is here.
